Maximum Temperature

Each dot represents a cityâ€™s maximum temperature on October 29th, 2020. The x-axis is the latitude. Zero is the equator. Negative values are latitudes in the Southern hemisphere, and positive values are latitudes in Northern hemisphere. The y-axis is the temperature in degrees Fahrenheit. In the Northern hemisphere, the values show a strong negative correlation, meaning as latitude increases, temperature decreases significantly. In the Southern hemisphere, this correlation is positive, but not nearly as strong as the Northern hemisphere. At about -10 degrees latitude and as the cities approach the equator, the cities the temperatures become about the same. The data clearly show that temperature increases as cities approach the equator, and that roughly the same temperature at the equator extends into the Southern hemisphere about 10 degrees before it starts dropping. So there is a similar equator temperature between 0 and -10 degrees which circles the globe.
